Introduction

This project offers a dive into three different asset classes, Invesco DB Precious Metals Fund, Bitcoin, and the Euro to US Dollar exchange rate, and their relation to a US economy benchmark, the Standard & Poor’s 500 Index. Each financial instrument has its own focus and puzzle piece in the economy. By seeking a relationship with the benchmark, a new perspective on the impact on the relationship between the asset classes and the US economy could represent a new investment strategy and business sentiment. To test this relationship, a regression will be conducted to test the R^2 value and the line best fit. Furthermore, there is a second hypotheses made that asks if there is a lag between the relationship between the benchmark and one of the asset classes. If a 6 month difference in the returns for both asset tools reveals a relationship, it could show the how investor sentiment and overall impact takes time on a relationship rather than an immediate connection. Following these regressions, there will be a “seaborn” visualization in attempt to differentiate the different markets and US economy on Python.

Chapter 1:

To start our analysis, we import differen't librabries to run code. Using the findings from Yahoo-Fiance, the indexes for all 4 markets describe the open, high, low, close, adjusted close and volume market price, associated with the 1st of every month, over the past five years. In order to clean the data for merging, we dropped columns that were not needed for each csv. Followed by this, we generated a returns column that took the current month closing price, subtracted by the following month, and divided by that current month for a percentage change for each csv file. This allowed data to be consistent for all 4 financial markets, for a simple merge on date.

After merging the returns of the SPX, DBP, BTC and EURUSD, for further analysis, we run a linear regression model, where our X is the returns of the S&P 500 for all 3 regressions and the Y differs based on the other financial markets examined. The linear regression used in this paper displays the relationship and trend for current to current month returns between the S&P 500 and the returns of the other financial markets. We replicate this regression 3 times for comparison in similarities, between the different markets used for analysis.

model = LinearRegression()
